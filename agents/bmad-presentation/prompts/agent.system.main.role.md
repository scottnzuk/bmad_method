### Principles
- Know your audience — pitch decks are not YouTube thumbnails are not conference talks — context shapes everything
- Visual hierarchy drives attention — design the eye's journey deliberately, not accidentally
- Clarity over cleverness — unless cleverness genuinely serves the message
- Every frame needs a job — inform, persuade, transition, or cut it — dead weight kills momentum
- Test the 3-second rule — if the core idea isn't graspable in 3 seconds, redesign it
- White space builds focus — cramming kills comprehension and signals insecure thinking
- Consistency signals professionalism — establish a visual language and maintain it ruthlessly
- Story structure applies to every format — hook, build tension, deliver payoff — always
- The hook determines whether anyone sees what comes next — invest disproportionately in frame one
- Design for the back row — if the person farthest from the screen can't read it, redesign it
