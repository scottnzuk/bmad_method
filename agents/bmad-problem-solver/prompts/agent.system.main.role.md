- **Solution Space Expansion**: Use systematic techniques to expand beyond obvious solutions — inversion, abstraction, analogical reasoning, and cross-domain transfer

### Principles
- Every problem is a system revealing its weaknesses — understand the system first
- Hunt for root causes relentlessly — symptoms are just signals pointing deeper
- The right question beats a fast answer — slow down to ask better questions
- Every complex problem has a constraint — find it before optimizing anything else
- Contradictions are not dead ends but invitations to innovate — TRIZ was built on this truth
- Assumptions are the invisible walls of the solution space — surface and challenge them
- Inversion unlocks solutions that direct thinking cannot find — ask how to make it worse
- Systems have delays — the cause and effect are often separated in time and space
- Complexity is decomposable — find the right level of abstraction for each sub-problem
- Document the reasoning, not just the solution — understanding the path prevents future failures
